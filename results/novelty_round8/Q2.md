## Q2

Here are the 5 most relevant, genuinely existing research items that utilize co-occurrence, co-activation, and related statistical measures to analyze and organize feature hierarchies, families, and absorption in Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs):

### 1. Sparse Autoencoders for Dense Text Embeddings Reveal Hierarchical Feature Sub-structure
* **Authors:** Charles O'Neill, Christine Ye, Kartheik Iyer, John F. Wu
* **Venue/Site:** NeurIPS 2024 / arXiv Preprint
* **Date:** August 2024
* **arXiv ID / URL:** [arXiv:2408.02622](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.02622)
* **Relation:** The authors construct a sparse block diagonal matrix based on feature co-activation patterns (how frequently features activate together on document datasets) to reveal distinct parent-child hierarchies and semantic "feature families."

### 2. The Geometry of Concepts: Sparse Autoencoder Feature Structure
* **Authors:** Eric J. Michaud, Yuxiao Li, David D. Baek, Joshua Engels, Xiaoqing Sun, Max Tegmark
* **Venue/Site:** Entropy 2025 / arXiv Preprint
* **Date:** October 2024 (revised March 2025)
* **arXiv ID / URL:** [arXiv:2410.19750](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.19750)
* **Relation:** Applies spectral clustering to feature co-occurrence statistics to identify "brain-scale" functional lobes (clusters of related concepts like coding or math), discovering that features which functionally co-occur are also closely geometrically aligned.

### 3. Tree SAE: Learning Hierarchical Feature Structures in Sparse Autoencoders
* **Authors:** Tue M. Cao, Hoang X. Nhat, Raed Alharbi, Phi Le Nguyen, My T. Thai
* **Venue/Site:** arXiv Preprint
* **Date:** May 2026
* **arXiv ID / URL:** [arXiv:2605.07922](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07922)
* **Relation:** Proposes Tree SAEs, which directly utilize co-activation rates and parent-child activation coverage constraints during training to resolve issues of feature splitting and feature absorption by enforcing hierarchical paths.

### 4. From Atoms to Trees: Building a Structured Feature Forest with Hierarchical Sparse Autoencoders
* **Authors:** Yifan Luo, Yang Zhan, Jiedong Jiang, Tianyang Liu, Mingrui Wu, Zhennan Zhou, Bin Dong
* **Venue/Site:** arXiv Preprint
* **Date:** February 2026
* **arXiv ID / URL:** [arXiv:2602.11881](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11881)
* **Relation:** Introduces Hierarchical Sparse Autoencoders (HSAE) that impose co-activation constraints representing parent-child probabilities to guide the learning of structured trees, resolving feature splitting into a unified, navigable forest.

### 5. A is for Absorption: Studying Feature Splitting and Absorption in Sparse Autoencoders
* **Authors:** David Chanin, James Wilken-Smith, Tomáš Dulka, Hardik Bhatnagar, Satvik Golechha, Joseph Bloom
* **Venue/Site:** NeurIPS 2024 / arXiv Preprint
* **Date:** September 2024
* **arXiv ID / URL:** [arXiv:2409.14507](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.14507)
* **Relation:** Uses Normalized Pointwise Mutual Information (NPMI) and co-occurrence rates to study "feature absorption"—a phenomenon where general parent concepts are pathologically absorbed into specific child latents to satisfy sparsity constraints.
